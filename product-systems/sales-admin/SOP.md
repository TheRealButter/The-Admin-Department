# Sales Admin SOP

## Daily routine

### 1. Check enquiry sources

Check all agreed sources:

- website forms
- WhatsApp messages
- Gmail/email
- Facebook/Instagram DMs if used
- phone-call notes
- referrals
- manual entries

Every enquiry must be logged in the Sales Admin tracker.

### 2. Capture required fields

Minimum fields:

- received date
- source
- contact name
- phone/email
- service needed
- location
- urgency
- status
- assigned owner
- first response status
- quote/follow-up due date
- next action

### 3. Prioritise urgency

Use:

- **High** — urgent request, ready to buy, breakdown/emergency, clear service need.
- **Normal** — serious enquiry, needs quote/info, not immediate.
- **Low** — vague enquiry, browsing, incomplete details.

### 4. First response rule

Every new lead must receive or be assigned a first response action on the same business day.

If the lead is high urgency, escalate to the owner immediately.

### 5. Follow-up rules

- Quote sent: follow up within 24–48 hours.
- Waiting for client: follow up after 2 business days.
- No response after 3 follow-ups: mark Cold, not Lost.
- Lost requires a reason where possible.

### 6. Status rules

Use one of:

- New
- Needs Response
- Contacted
- Waiting for Client
- Quote Needed
- Quote Sent
- Follow-up Due
- Won
- Lost
- Cold
- Closed

### 7. Weekly report

Every week, report:

- leads received
- leads contacted
- leads still needing response
- quotes sent
- overdue follow-ups
- won leads
- lost/cold leads
- estimated value of open quotes

## Escalation rules

Escalate if:

- high-urgency lead is older than 1 hour without response
- quote over threshold needs owner review
- client says they are ready to pay/book
- complaint or angry prospect appears
- lead source produces repeated low-quality enquiries

## Quality checklist

- no lead remains New overnight
- every Quote Sent lead has a follow-up due date
- every Lost lead has a reason where possible
- every lead has an assigned owner
- weekly report includes overdue actions
