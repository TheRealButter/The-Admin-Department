# Sales Admin Demo Walkthrough

Use this as a 5-minute sales demo.

## 1. Open with the pain

Say:

> Most businesses are not losing leads because they do not care. They lose leads because enquiries arrive from too many places and follow-ups depend on memory.

Ask:

- Where do your enquiries come from now?
- Who replies first?
- How do you know which quotes need follow-up?
- What happens when the owner is busy?
- How many leads go cold because nobody chased them?

## 2. Show the dashboard

Open `demo-dashboard.html`.

Point out:

- leads received
- leads needing response
- quotes sent
- follow-ups due
- open quote value

Say:

> The first goal is visibility. Every enquiry needs a status, owner, and next action.

## 3. Show the lead tracker

Explain each example:

- Thabo Mokoena: urgent lead not yet contacted.
- Lerato Naidoo: quote sent and follow-up due soon.
- Ayesha Khan: high-value quote needs same-day follow-up.
- Chris Botha: waiting for client photos.
- Pieter Jacobs: won lead moved to scheduling.

Say:

> This is how we stop leads from living only in WhatsApp and memory.

## 4. Show the follow-up queue

Open `follow-up-queue.csv`.

Say:

> The follow-up queue is where money is protected. It tells the team exactly who needs contact, why, and by when.

## 5. Show the weekly report

Open `filled-weekly-report.md`.

Say:

> At the end of the week, the owner can see what came in, what was quoted, what was won, what is still open, and what follow-ups are overdue.

## 6. Explain what setup includes

Say:

> We install the enquiry tracker, lead statuses, follow-up rules, message templates, and weekly report. We start manual and controlled. Once the process is working, we can add reminders or click-to-send messages.

## 7. Close

Say:

> The best next step is to run this against your current enquiry process. We can start with the last 10–20 leads and show exactly where follow-ups are being lost.

## Common objections

### “We already use WhatsApp.”

Response:

> Good. We are not replacing WhatsApp. We are adding structure around it so enquiries and follow-ups do not disappear inside chats.

### “We already have a spreadsheet.”

Response:

> That helps. We will clean the workflow around the spreadsheet: statuses, owners, due dates, follow-up rules, and reports.

### “Will this send messages automatically?”

Response:

> Not at the start. The first version prepares and tracks the work. Your team still approves and sends messages.

### “Is this a CRM?”

Response:

> It is not trying to be a big CRM. It is a practical sales admin system for businesses that need to stop missing enquiries and follow-ups.
